The queue that outgrew itself.

A post-mortem on the failure mode nobody saw coming — and the one line of config that made it go away.

24 min read visual-explainer
01 · Opening

What looked like a spike turned out to be a ceiling.

The dashboard said throughput was fine. The queue depth graph said otherwise. Between the two, we lost three customers before anyone reconciled the gap.

Before
500 rps

Throughput ceiling. Not the average — the ceiling. Past this point the queue depth climbed linearly until workers OOM'd.

After
4,200 rps

Throughput ceiling after partitioning by tenant rather than by message type. 8.4× the previous headroom, same hardware.

throughput in production

Regressions

Zero so far.
