01
Overview Updated 2026-04-16

Three stages, one latency budget.

The pipeline accepts 16 kHz mono PCM on an open WebSocket, segments the stream into phrases, emits incremental transcripts as they stabilize, feeds each stable phrase to a small instruct model, and returns synthesized audio back across the same socket. The round-trip budget is 220 ms at the 50th percentile and 480 ms at the 95th.

Each stage is a separately deployable service. The orchestrator in this document is the thin JSON-RPC shim in front of them — not a runtime. Teams swap models for stages individually; the contract between stages is stable.

03
Implementation Minimum viable client

The smallest thing that opens the socket.

This is the minimum viable TypeScript client. The pipeline expects 16 kHz mono PCM in 20 ms frames. Everything else — voice activity detection, barge-in handling, audio ducking — runs on the orchestrator, not the client.

Design note

The contract is the socket, not the SDK.

The TypeScript SDK above is a convenience wrapper — nothing more. Every event it surfaces corresponds to a framed JSON message on the wire. Teams shipping in Go, Rust, or Python open the socket directly and read the same framing spec. The SDK is not a source of truth; the WebSocket protocol is.

05
Next Owners and timing

Three threads open for v0.5.

The LLM first-token budget is approaching its ceiling — that's the thread to pull next. Two smaller investigations sit behind it: a tokenizer swap on the speech side that should release ~12 ms at p50, and a streaming audio codec change on the synthesis side that closes the stereo gap without inflating bandwidth.

01 / LLM first-token headroom

KV-cache warming plus speculative decoding. Targeted ceiling: 180 ms at p95. Owner: platform. Readiness review: 2026-05-02.

02 / Tokenizer swap on speech side

Byte-level BPE tuned on call-center-domain audio. Expected gain: ~12 ms at p50 on streaming stability. Owner: speech. Readiness review: 2026-05-09.

03 / Stereo synthesis without bandwidth cost

Opus frames at 32 kbps with mid-side encoding. Subjective tests on the way. Owner: synthesis. Readiness review: 2026-05-16.
